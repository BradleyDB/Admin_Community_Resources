# Briefing Format

Write in an executive narrative voice — direct, specific, actionable. Each section should read like a well-informed colleague briefing the exec, not a system report. The reader has 60 seconds before their week starts; every sentence has to earn its place.

## Document order (Canvas or DM text)

1. **Title**: `CS Executive Briefing — Week of {Monday's date}`
2. **Status Legend** — immediately after the title:

   ```
   ---
   **Status Legend**
   🔴 Confirmed Churn / Churning · 🟠 At Risk · 🟡 Watching · 🟢 Recovering · 🌑 Gone Dark · 🌱 Expansion Signal
   ---
   ```

3. **⚡ 3 Things You Need to Know This Week** (TL;DR block):
   - 🔴 **Top Risk:** one sentence — the single most critical risk: named account, ARR, why it matters now.
   - 🌱 **Top Opportunity:** one sentence — the most actionable expansion signal: named account or CSM, ARR, what's ready to close.
   - ✅ **Critical Question This Week:** one sentence — the most important question: named manager, named account, specific ask.
4. Sections 1–6 below, in order.
5. **Footer**, exactly:

   ```
   Generated by Claude + Staircase AI · Next scheduled run: {day}, {next run date} at {delivery time} {timezone}
   ```

   If Staircase admin notes are relevant (e.g., unresolved duplicate IDs), add them on a separate line below the footer.

## Continuity language

Use history context wherever applicable: *"Flagged for the third consecutive week — no re-engagement plan confirmed."* / *"Sentiment recovered from 10 → 34 since last week's flag — recovery call appears to be working."* / *"Previously flagged — marking resolved."* On Week 1 (no history), omit continuity language entirely and omit Section 3 entirely — no header, no placeholder.

## Sections

### Section 1 — 📊 What's Happening Across the Segment

Aggregate themes across the full book — never account by account. What patterns show up in customer conversations this week? Which product areas, topics, or sentiment trends appear across multiple CSMs or pods? What's the overall tone of the business? **Max 3 sentences per theme**: lead with the pattern, state the ARR exposure, give the one-line implication.

### Section 2 — 🔴 New Risks This Week

Only accounts where something **materially changed this week** — previously flagged accounts belong in Section 3. For each:

- Account name (with Staircase link) + status emoji (criteria below)
- CSM (account owner) and manager
- ARR, health score, sentiment score
- One-sentence summary of what changed
- CSM presence status (from presence-detection.md)
- **One** verbatim customer quote, max — pick the one that most clearly expresses emotional state or stated intent: a direct consequence, a decision, explicit sentiment. Skip procedural/logistical quotes. *"I do not trust you"* lands; "Sentiment Score: 7" doesn't.

**Status emoji criteria:**

| Status | Emoji | When |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmed Churn / Churning | 🔴 | Formal non-renewal submitted, deactivation confirmed, or explicit stated intent to leave — regardless of scores |
| At Risk | 🟠 | Active escalation, evaluating alternatives, sentiment < 20, health < 20, or renewal within 90 days with unresolved blockers |
| Recovering | 🟢 | Renewal signed **and** no material blockers remaining |
| Watching | 🟡 | Previously flagged, no new deterioration, static |

A renewal signature alone doesn't make an account 🟢 — if post-close blockers remain, keep it 🟠.

### Section 3 — ⚠️ Still Watching *(Week 2+)*

Previously flagged accounts with no resolution. For each: consecutive weeks open; whether the signal is **improving**, **worsening**, or **static**; updated health/sentiment if changed; a new verbatim quote if one is available (one max).

### Section 4 — 🌑 Accounts Gone Dark

No customer-initiated engagement for 2+ weeks — early churn warnings. **Max 2 lines per entry**: account name + Staircase link, ARR, CSM, manager, last engagement date; note if previously flagged and for how long.

### Section 5 — 🌱 Expansion Signals

New locations, upsell interest, NPS promoters, positive sentiment spikes. **One line per account**: account, CSM, manager, signal summary — and flag any signal sitting unactioned from a prior week.

### Section 6 — ✅ Questions to Ask This Week

2–3 grounded questions per relevant manager, each tied to a specific account or signal from this briefing. **Max 2 sentences each**: the setup and the ask. Tone is friendly and curious — checking in, not escalating; the goal is to arm the exec for their manager syncs, not to put managers on trial.

**One email per manager.** Consolidate all of a manager's questions into a single mailto link:

- **Subject:** the most critical account, plus "— and a couple of other things" if there are multiple questions
- **Body:** friendly one-liner open → each question as a short paragraph → "Thanks, {Recipient First Name}." Max 6–8 sentences total.
- Build addresses from the org email format in `_org.json` (honor exceptions and aliases; hyphenated last names keep the full hyphenated form).

Format per manager:

> **[Manager Name](mailto:manager.email@company.com?subject=...&body=...)** — one-line summary of what the email covers.

When delivering to Canvas, open the section with this italic line: *💡 Click a manager's name to open a pre-written email ready to send.* (Omit for DM-text delivery — mailto links aren't clickable there.)

## Canvas formatting

`#` for section headers · `**bold**` for account names, scores, key labels · `-` for bullets · `>` for verbatim quotes · keep all Staircase links clickable · URL-encode mailto subject/body.
